## Initial understanding
- Core market model: price alternates between regional movement (balance/range) and migration (directional trend).
- Primary structural observation is on monthly/weekly/daily charts; lower timeframes are used mainly for execution, objective small stops, and short-term protection.
- Breakouts of important boundaries or critical connections can start migration.
- The system separates trend handling from range handling.
- Trend handling emphasizes holding, pyramiding when conditions support it, and protecting profit with progressively tighter trendlines or other objective protection.
- Range handling emphasizes objective boundaries, objective small stops, and shorter holding.
- Trade quality is linked to objective reward, objective loss, and estimated success/failure probability rather than prediction alone.
- The example collection shows context-dependent holding: take a small move and leave in unstable or defensive conditions; hold longer when new space, strong directional movement, or higher-timeframe confirmation appears.

## Important caution
The files contain a mixture of theory, forum questions, participant interpretations, examples, and later refinements. Not every sentence is an official final rule. Rules must be classified as author statement, example, participant suggestion, or unresolved interpretation before coding.
