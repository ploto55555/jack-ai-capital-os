## Episode I — Previous-Day High/Low Breakout

- Core tools: Support, Resistance, Trendline.
- Primary reference timeframe: Daily.
- Yesterday's high is Resistance; yesterday's low is Support.
- Long after price penetrates yesterday's high.
- Short after price penetrates yesterday's low.
- No penetration means no trade.
- Pending orders may be used with spread plus 1-2 pips as buffer.
- Trendline provides directional context.
- Avoid trades near important monthly or weekly highs/lows.
- Prefer low-spread pairs and limit simultaneous pairs.
- Source examples mention 5-10 pip TP and 20-30 pip SL, but these are not yet validated universal parameters.

## Episode II — Trend and Channel Extension

- Episode II enters earlier than Episode I; the previous candle S/R may become T2 instead of the entry trigger.
- Directional tools: FX Sniper's Ergodic CCI & Trigger, SHI Channel on D1, and lower-timeframe confirmation.
- CCI rule: blue above red = bullish; red above blue = bearish.
- Channel structure: top, median and bottom.
- Expected channel path: median to outer channel, then back toward median, though the source presents this as a tendency rather than a guarantee.
- At the D1 median, H1 is used for confirmation:
  - H1 closes/forms below median = bearish sign.
  - H1 closes/forms above median = bullish sign.
  - H1 touches median = indecision; wait.
- Exit is split into T1, T2 and T3.
- Example allocation: T1 70%, T2 20%, T3 10%.
- T2 may use yesterday's support/resistance.
- T3 may use the D1 channel median or another extended target.
- Example GBPUSD stop range: 50-60 pips; this is source-specific and not yet validated universally.
- Example case moves stop to breakeven after the first entry objective rather than moving take-profit levels.

## Episode III — 00:00-08:00 GMT Box Breakout

- Build a box from 00:00 GMT to 08:00 GMT using the high and low of those eight hours.
- H1 is used to construct the box; M15 is used for earlier breakout detection and execution.
- Three source entry styles:
  1. Enter immediately when price penetrates the box.
  2. Enter at the opening of a new candle after penetration, provided the candle opens outside the box.
  3. Wait for a fully formed M15 candle outside the box, then enter at the next candle open.
- Programmatic full-candle condition:
  - Bullish breakout: M15 candle low > box top.
  - Bearish breakout: M15 candle high < box bottom.
- Source exit ideas:
  - 20 pips is described as easy, 40 pips as sweet, and sometimes 100 pips.
  - TP may be estimated as session range minus box size.
  - Trailing lock may be two-thirds of that value.
  - Time exit around London close: 16:00 or 17:00 GMT depending on daylight saving.
- Episode I filter:
  - Yesterday's daily S/R is treated as stronger than the Episode III box.
  - When box breakout and daily S/R breakout align, the source considers it a stronger event.
- Episode II filter:
  - Use channel position and trend direction to choose the more likely breakout side.
  - Channel top/bottom may serve as final TP.
- Trendsetter:
  - 84SMA on M15.
  - 00:00 GMT opening candle above 84SMA = uptrend; below 84SMA = downtrend.
- Source stop options:
  1. Box size.
  2. Fixed 50 pips for GBPUSD example.
  3. M15 84SMA or H1 21SMA.
- Both-direction entries are allowed when the box is near the channel median; otherwise the trend direction is preferred.

## Research interpretation

SRDC is not one single rule. It is a three-layer framework:

1. Episode I: previous-day high/low breakout reference.
2. Episode II: directional and channel context with earlier entries and staged exits.
3. Episode III: London-session box breakout with Episode I and II filters.

The following source values are examples, not approved parameters: fixed TP, fixed SL, 50-pip stop, 84SMA, 21SMA, CCI settings, 70/20/10 exit allocation, range-minus-box-size target, and London-close exit. They must be tested independently by pair, timezone, data source, session definition, spread, slippage, volatility regime and entry style.
