## Verified SRDC core from the uploaded DOC

- Uses only Support, Resistance, and Trendline.
- Primary reference timeframe is Daily.
- Yesterday's candle high is Resistance.
- Yesterday's candle low is Support.
- Long when price penetrates yesterday's high.
- Short when price penetrates yesterday's low.
- No penetration means no trade.
- Backtest at least 20 daily candles and count post-breakout pip movement after spread.
- Pending orders may be used.
- Add spread plus 1-2 pips as a buffer to avoid execution exactly on the line.
- Trendline is used to estimate directional bias.
- Avoid entries near major monthly or weekly highs and lows.
- Prefer low-spread pairs and avoid monitoring too many pairs.
- The source mentions 5 or 10 pip take-profit examples and a commonly used 20-30 pip stop, but these are source examples rather than validated universal settings.

## Research interpretation

SRDC should be treated first as a simple previous-day high/low breakout engine. Trendline, higher-timeframe extremes, spread, and execution buffer are filters or implementation details. The original source does not establish that fixed 5/10 pip targets or 20-30 pip stops are universally optimal. These values require separate testing by pair, session, volatility regime, and transaction cost.
